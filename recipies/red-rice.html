Descritpion

Rice is an important part of Mexican diet. Mexican rice differs from every other traditional rice preparation because, as with everything, we adapt it to or ingredients and taste. Mexican red rice is one great example of how a very common dish around the world such as rice gets mexicanized.

Making mexican red rice is very simple! The key difference is that, instead of boiling the rice in plain or salted water, we boil it in a tomato broth which gives the color and taste to the rice. It is very common to it this version of rice as a side dish or as an extra ingredient in tacos. Let's get to it!

Ingredients

  • Rice (any kind, mind cooking time depending on the type of rice)
  • Vegetable oil
  • Roma tomatoes
  • White Onion
  • Garlic
  • Powdered chicken stock
  • Assorted frozen veggies

Instructions

  1. Prepare your rice: If you are not using instant rice, you should first wash, rinse, and dry your rice. Once it is ready to be cooked, set it aside.
  2. Prepare the tomato paste: Blend your tomatoes, garlic, and onion. Add some powdered chicken stock to the blend for seasoning.
  3. Boil the tomato paste until it gets reduced: Put your tomato paste in a hot pot with oil. Let the paste boil until it thickens.
  4. Add water and cook the rice: Once the paste is reduced, add water and taste it to verify it is well seasoned. Add one cup per cup of rice if you are using instant rice, otherwise add two cups per cup of rice. Once the water comes to a boil, add your rice and let it cook at low heat until the water evaporates.
  5. Add the frozen veggies: Once the rice is almost cooked, you can add your veggies. If they were forzen, make sure to first defrost them and rinse them. Mix until the veggies are evenly distributed. Serve and enjoy!
